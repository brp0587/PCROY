Prafulla Chandra Ray

Acharya Prafulla Chandra Ray

A pioneer of modern Indian chemistry, a visionary industrialist, and a true nationalist who tirelessly worked for the scientific and economic upliftment of India.

Early Life and Education

Born in Khulna, Bengal Presidency (now in Bangladesh), Prafulla Chandra Ray was a brilliant student who developed a deep interest in science from a young age. He earned a scholarship to study at the University of Edinburgh in Scotland, where he obtained his D.Sc. in Chemistry. His European education broadened his perspective and strengthened his resolve to build a strong scientific base in India.

Upon his return, he became a professor at the Presidency College in Calcutta. Here, in a small, poorly equipped laboratory, he began his groundbreaking research and established what would become the first modern school of chemistry in India.

Groundbreaking Scientific Contributions

Dr. Ray's most notable scientific achievement was the discovery and synthesis of **mercurous nitrite** in 1896. At the time, this compound was considered unstable and impossible to isolate. His success was a major feat, which he announced with the chemical formula:

Hg2(NO2)2

This discovery was published in the prestigious 'Journal of the Chemical Society' in London, bringing international recognition to Indian science. It opened up a new field of study into nitrites and inspired a generation of his students to pursue careers in chemical research, laying the foundation for modern chemistry in India.

A Visionary Entrepreneur and Educator

Prafulla Chandra Ray was more than just a scientist; he was an entrepreneur with a vision for industrial self-reliance. Disheartened by India's dependence on foreign medicines, he founded the **Bengal Chemical & Pharmaceutical Works (BCPW)** in 1901 with his own modest savings. BCPW was India's first indigenous pharmaceutical company, and it became a huge success, producing affordable medicines and chemicals.

His most acclaimed literary work, **"The History of Hindu Chemistry,"** is a two-volume masterpiece that meticulously documented ancient Indian texts to highlight the rich chemical knowledge of ancient India. This work challenged the colonial-era belief that India lacked a scientific heritage, instilling a sense of pride and confidence in the nation's scientific community.

A Lasting Legacy

Acharya Prafulla Chandra Ray's legacy is immense. He is rightly known as the **"Father of Indian Chemistry"** for his scientific discoveries, his role as a mentor to countless scientists (including Satyendra Nath Bose and Meghnad Saha), and for creating a vibrant scientific community. His students went on to become prominent scientists, carrying forward his vision for a scientifically advanced India.

His life's work demonstrated that science, industry, and patriotism could go hand-in-hand. He remains a towering figure who laid the groundwork for India's modern scientific and industrial growth.
